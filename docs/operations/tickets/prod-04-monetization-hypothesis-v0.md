# PROD-04 Monetization Scope and Pricing Hypothesis (v0)

Last updated: 2026-03-04
Owner: Product Lead
Status: In Progress

## Objective

Define a beta-safe monetization hypothesis that can be validated in parallel with engineering runtime closure.

## Constraints

1. No paid plan claim goes public before ENG-13 closure evidence is accepted.
2. Beta access remains governed by rollout quality and support capacity.
3. Pricing assumptions must map to validated MVP capabilities only.

## Draft Packaging Hypothesis

1. `Free Beta`: capped daily usage, feedback required.
2. `Pro Tester` (post-beta candidate): higher usage caps, priority support, early feature gates.
3. `Team Pilot` (post-beta candidate): small-seat plan for structured feedback groups.

## Pricing Signal Hypothesis (for testing)

1. Primary value anchors:
   - local-first privacy
   - offline reliability
   - practical utility workflows
2. Willingness-to-pay is expected to correlate more with reliability consistency than with raw benchmark speed.

## Validation Plan (Parallel to ENG-13)

1. Interview 10-15 qualified beta applicants.
2. Run 2 pricing sensitivity prompts in onboarding follow-up.
3. Capture objections by taxonomy: trust, reliability, utility, support.
4. Produce one-page recommendation with go/no-go for paid beta experiment.

## Decision Triggers

1. Proceed to limited paid experiment only if:
   - first-week activation quality is stable,
   - support burden remains within WP-09 threshold,
   - no major trust/privacy objections emerge.
2. Otherwise, continue free beta and re-evaluate after next evidence cycle.

## Outputs

1. Interview summary with willingness-to-pay bands.
2. Recommended packaging for post-beta launch.
3. Required product/legal/policy follow-ups.
